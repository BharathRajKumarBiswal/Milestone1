Combat Desertification

Preventing drought through land restoration

Land Management Practices:

Implement sustainable land management practices, such as agroforestry, contour plowing, and cover cropping, to enhance soil fertility, prevent erosion, and promote water retention.

Dryland restoration:

CIFOR-ICRAF scientists are helping to combat deforestation and biodiversity loss through targeted research and engagement through our work on restoration, sustainable forest management, soil and land health and tree genetic resources.

Water Conservation:

Implement water conservation measures, including efficient irrigation techniques, rainwater harvesting, and the restoration of natural water sources. Proper water management helps maintain soil moisture levels and prevents desertification.

Community Engagement and Education:

Involve local communities in sustainable land use practices, providing them with education and training on soil conservation, reforestation, and water management. Community engagement fosters a sense of ownership and ensures the long-term success of desertification prevention efforts.

Reforestation and Afforestation:

Undertake large-scale reforestation and afforestation programs to increase vegetation cover, combat soil degradation, and restore ecosystems. Planting native and drought-resistant species can be particularly effective.
